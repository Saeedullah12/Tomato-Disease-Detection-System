About

Overview :

The tomato (Solanum lycopersicum) is a versatile fruit from the nightshade family, commonly used as a vegetable. Native to South America, it is rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants like lycopene. Cultivated worldwide, tomatoes vary in shape, size, and color. They are used fresh in salads and salsas, cooked in sauces and soups, and preserved. Tomatoes are praised for their health benefits, including improved heart health and reduced cancer risk.

Disease Prediction

Diagnoses

Tomato Bacterial Spot

Treatment :
Use copper-based bactericides .
Plant resistant varieties .
Treat seeds with hot water or bleach solution before planting .
Practice crop rotation and avoid overhead irrigation .

Tomato Early Blight

Treatment :
Apply fungicides (chlorothalonil, copper, mancozeb) .
Plant resistant varieties .
Rotate crops and avoid overhead watering .
Remove and destroy infected plant .

Tomato Healthy

Symptoms of Healthy Tomato :
Leaves: Vibrant green, no spots .
Stems: Thick and sturdy .
Growth: Steady and uniform .
Flowers: Bright and well-formed .
Fruit: Smooth, even-colored .
Pests: No visible damage .

Tomato Late Blight

Treatment :
Apply fungicides like chlorothalonil .
Choose resistant tomato varieties .
Improve air circulation and avoid overhead watering .
Remove and destroy infected plants promptly .

Tomato Leaf Mold

Treatment :
Apply fungicides containing copper or chlorothalonil preventatively .
Improve air circulation and avoid overhead watering .
Prune and dispose of infected leaves to reduce disease spread .

Tomato Septoria Leaf

Treatment :
Apply fungicides containing chlorothalonil or copper .
Prune and dispose of infected leaves to reduce disease spread .
Rotate crops to prevent buildup of pathogens in soil .

Tomato Spider Mites

Treatment :
Use predatory mites (e.g., Phytoseiulus persimilis) to control spider mite populations .
Apply horticultural oils to suffocate mites and their eggs .
Use insecticidal soaps to kill mites on contact .
